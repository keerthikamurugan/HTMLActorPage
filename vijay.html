Vijay

Chandrasekaran Joseph Vijay (born 22 June 1974) is an Indian actor and politician. He is the president of the political party Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam. In the 2026 Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly election, his party secured 108 seats, marking a significant political achievement. In a career spanning over three decades in Tamil cinema, Vijay appeared in 69 films and ranked among India's highest-paid actors. He made his debut as a child actor in the film Vetri (1984). He had his first lead role in the film Naalaiya Theerpu (1992) directed by his father. He gained fame with the romance films Poove Unakkaga (1996), Love Today (1997), Kadhalukku Mariyadhai (1997), Thullatha Manamum Thullum (1999), Kushi (2000), Priyamaanavale (2000) and Friends (2001), before transitioning to action with commercial successes such as Thirumalai (2003), Ghilli (2004), Thirupaachi (2005) and Pokkiri (2007). From the early 2010s, he starred in critically and commercially successful films including Thuppakki (2012), Kaththi (2014), Mersal (2017), Sarkar (2018), Master (2021), Leo (2023), and The Greatest of All Time (2024). Several rank among the highest-grossing Tamil films, earning him numerous awards and nominations.
